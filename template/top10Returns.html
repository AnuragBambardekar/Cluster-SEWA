Top 10 Companies based on Returns Volatility Ratio.

The return-volatility ratio, also known as the Sharpe Ratio, is an important metric in finance because it measures the risk-adjusted return of an investment. It is calculated by taking the difference between the investment's return and the risk-free rate of return, and dividing that by the investment's volatility (standard deviation of returns). The higher the Sharpe Ratio, the better the investment's risk-adjusted performance. The Sharpe Ratio is important because it helps investors compare the performance of different investments on a risk-adjusted basis. Two investments may have the same return, but if one has lower volatility, it may be a better investment because it has a higher Sharpe Ratio. Additionally, the Sharpe Ratio can be used to optimize a portfolio by choosing investments that have high expected returns and low volatility. By maximizing the Sharpe Ratio of a portfolio, investors can achieve a higher return for a given level of risk, or a lower risk for a given level of return.
